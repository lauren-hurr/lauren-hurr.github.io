Project Scope

Add a new feature to an existing app

Overview

With the rising prices of ridesharing services, calling a ride is seen as a luxury nowadays. Lyft has made efforts to alleviate the high costs by adding services such as Lyft Share that allow people to split rides with strangers to lower their individual costs. However, many users expressed negative sentiments towards riding with strangers as well as other issues within the app. My goal was to solve user pain points by adding a new feature. The new “Add Rider” feature is a way for Lyft riders to share rides with their friends and split payments within the app, which makes rides more affordable and enjoyable.

Since I mainly use Lyft for ridesharing, I wanted to add a feature to a familiar app and challenge myself to adapt to an existing style guide.

Disclaimer: This is a personal project. I do not work for or have any affiliation with Lyft.

The Challenge

How might we make Lyft rides more enjoyable and affordable?

01.


Research

Empathizing with Lyft Riders

User Interviews

In order to empathize with other Lyft users, I conducted user interviews with 5 participants. I wanted to understand their real experiences while using Lyft and how they are utilizing the features Lyft currently offers. I created an interview guide, which listed open-ended questions, that helped me facilitate the interview.

02.


Synthesize

Identifying User Pain Points

Affinity Mapping, User Persona

To analyze the qualitative data that I gathered through user interviews, I used an affinity map to find common themes among the users’ feedback. I divided it up into three sections to see how users behave, how they respond, and how they use the application’s features.

Focusing on what seemed the most feasible, I identified three pain points of the users’ experiences: lack of affordability, clunky split payment process, and desynchronized arrival times. I created a user persona to embody the key audience that would benefit the most from this new feature: a social, young professional in a large city. Creating a user persona helped to tackle the various pain points and needs generated from user research into a single "person" that I can easily reference during the design process.

03.


Ideate

Brainstorming Solutions

Mind Mapping, Competitive analysis

I brainstormed solutions by creating a mind map that laid out potential solutions to the user persona’s pain points. To guide me in creating more solutions, I completed a competitive analysis to see how other ridesharing apps solved similar issues and to see if any of my ideas had already been implemented. I chose to move forward with the idea of adding a friend to a Lyft ride, similar to carpooling. Since this proposed feature would tackle all three pain points, it was the most well-rounded and practical solution.

Structuring The Experience

Task Flow, User Flow, Sketches

Once I decided on my idea of adding an “Add Rider” feature, I created a task flow to map out the steps the user will take to utilize this new feature. This was challenging as a new feature must not feel out of place in the current flow, and creating a framework was important for sketching out the screens. Stepping into the persona's shoes, I created a user flow that mapped out the decision-making points to ensure that the flow is intuitive.

04.


Prototype

Prototyping, Testing, and Refining the Solution

Hi-Fi Prototype, Usability Testing

Using my sketches, I created a hi-fi prototype to test the new “Add Rider” feature using Lyft's existing style guidelines. My main goal was to validate the potential demand for this new feature as well as quickly assess and uncover any usability issues I may have overlooked. I gathered 8 participants and utilized Maze, a rapid prototyping tool, and discovered a few usability issues within the prototype. I then brainstormed solutions and made several adjustments to directly address user’s feedback.

05.


Validate

Solving Usability Bugs

Usability Testing

I conducted a second usability test with 8 new participants and found that the redesigns directly addressed the usability issues uncovered from the first test. I found there was a large improvement in several usability metrics, including misclick rate, average duration, and bounce rate. The users were able to complete the tasks more quickly, with a higher success rate.

Add Another Rider to Your Ride

Lyft riders can now add their friends to their ride directly through the Lyft app. Riders no longer need to ride with strangers to reduce the cost of the ride. Now, they can ride with their friends for a more affordable and enjoyable ride. No more worries about arriving at drastically different times, since riders will be picked up by the same driver and dropped off at the same time.

Easily Split the Cost of the Ride

Riders can easily split the cost with friends without leaving the app. Just simply click on "Split fare" to request to split the cost with the additional rider. Payment splitting is automatically calculated evenly.

Reflection

Since Lyft invests a lot into their user experience, they left very few stones unturned. Gathering data from interviews and competitor apps were integral to developing my proposed feature. The user testing was also a key learning experience as I was able to fix usability issues that I had overlooked. At first glance, implementing the "Add Rider" feature placement may look simple. In actuality, it was challenging to work within the style guide constraints and to figure out where exactly the new feature would be seamlessly integrated.
