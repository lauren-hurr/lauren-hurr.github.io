Lyft

The new “Add Rider” feature on Lyft lets you add your friends to your ride and split payment directly through the app.

Main Goal

Add a feature to help users easily split rides with friends

Overview

The “Add a Rider” feature is a way for riders to share rides with their friends through the app, reducing communication outside the app and the use for other apps.

Since I only use Lyft for ridesharing, I wanted to add a feature to a familar app. Also, I wanted to challenge myself to adapt to an existing style guide.

Disclaimer: I do not work for or have any affiliation with Lyft.

The Challenge

How might we make Lyft rides more enjoyable and affordable?

01.


Research

Empathizing with Lyft Riders

User Interviews

In order to gain empathy and really understand the target user’s needs and pain points, I conducted user interviews with regular Lyft users to understand their experience with the app and how they are utilizing the features that Lyft provides.

02.


Synthesize

Identifying User Pain Points

Affinity Mapping, User Persona

To analyze this qualitative data, I used an affinity map to find common themes among the users’ feedback. I divided it up into three sections to see how users behave, how they respond, and how they use the application’s features. Focusing on what seemed the most feasible, I identified three pain points of the users’ experiences: lack of affordability, clunky split payment, and desynced arrival times. I created a user persona to embody the user I will be creating the new feature for.

03.


Ideate

Brainstorming Solutions

Competitive analysis

I brainstormed solutions on pen and paper that would tackle the user persona’s pain points. I completed a competitive analysis, to see how other ridesharing apps solved similar issues and to see if the ideas I’ve come up with has already been implemented. I chose to move forward with the idea of adding a friend to a Lyft ride, similar to carpooling. Since this proposed feature would tackle all three pain points, it was the most well-rounded solution.

Building it Out/Structuring The Experience

Task Flow, User Flow, Sketches

Once I decided on my idea of adding an “Add Rider” feature, I created a task flow to understand what the flow would be. This was challenging as a new feature must not feel out of place in the current flow, and creating a framework was important for sketching out the screens. I also created a user flow to establish the various decision making points a user will go through while using the new feature.

04.


Prototype

Prototyping, Testing, and Refining the Solution

Hi-fi Prototype

Using my sketches, I created a hi-fi prototype to test the new “Add Rider” feature. My main goal was to validate the potential demand for this new feature as well as quickly assess and uncover any usability issues I may have overlooked. I gathered 8 participants and utilized Maze, a rapid prototyping tool, and discovered a few usability issues within the prototype. I then brainstormed solutions and made several adjustments to directly address the user’s feedback.

05.


Validate

Solving usability bugs

Usability Testing

I conducted a second usability test with 8 new participants and found that the redesigns directly addressed the usability issues uncovered from the first test. I found there was a large improvement in several usability metrics, including misclick rate, average duration, and bounce rate. The users were able to complete the tasks more quickly, with a higher success rate.

Add another ride to your ride

Riders can now add their friends to their ride directly through the Lyft app. Rider no longer need to ride with strangers to reduce the cost of the ride and will arrive to the destination together.

Easily Split the Cost of the Ride

Riders can easily split the cost with friends without leaving the app. Payment splitting is automatically calculated.

Reflection

Since Lyft invests a lot into their user experience, they left very few stones unturned. Gathering data from interviews and competitor apps were integral to developing my proposed feature. The user testing was also a key learning experience as I was able to fix usability issues that I had overlooked.
