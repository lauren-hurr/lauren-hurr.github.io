The Problem

Kaus is struggling to reach younger audiences.
The complexity of products make it difficult for inexperienced purchasers to understand.
The abundance of options make the website hard to navigate.

The Solution

Rebrand to appeal towards younger audiences.
Redesign the information architecture for easy browsability.
Streamline the quote process.

01.


Research

Secondary Research

Knowing nothing about insurance, I first conducted secondary research to understand how insurance works and how people bought insurance in the U.S. Once I got a good grasp of the overall insurance industry, I delved deeper into insurance trends and consumer buying behaviors to deepen my understanding of the problem space

Key findings

73.7% of uninsured adults said that they were uninsured because the cost of coverage was too high.
29% of insurance customers are satisfied with their current providers.
49% of the U.S. total population receive employer-sponsored health insurance.

Competitive Analysis

Now that I have a better understanding of the insurance space, I conducted a competitive analysis to uncover competitor’s strengths and weaknesses to help establish Kaus’ unique value proposition.

Most insurance companies only sell insurance through an agent; only 1 company offers users to buy insurance online
2/5 competitors offer bundling options

User Interviews

I conducted three 45 minute interviews to get an in-depth understanding of people’s experiences purchasing insurance to identify pain points, as well as desires.

“There were a lot of uneccesarry steps when I purchased my insurance”
“It’s honestly so insane how much they charge for pet insurance.”
“The process seems really intimidating and I don’t even know where to start”
“The price is the most important when choosing an insurance.”

Key findings

Cost of insurance is the main factor when deciding on an insurance policy for all three participants.
All participants find insurance terminology confusing and hard to learn.
Researching and getting a quote is viewed as the most tedious part of the process by all three participants

03.


Ideate

Card Sorting

To help me determine the Information Architecture for the top navigation, I conducted a remote card sort utilizing OptimalSort with the help of 4 voluntary participants. After I reviewed the card sorting results, I compared my findings to that of the competitors’ and found a few patterns in how insurance information is grouped.

Site Map

I organized the results from the card sort and created a site map for the main navigation. I structured the information to be intuitive and easily browsable from the perspective of the user persona.

Task Flow

I created a task flow using the user persona as the main character. The task flow helped me understand the path the user is going to task to complete the main task. My goal was to find the main touch points and possible usability bugs.

User Flow

Creating a user flow helped me to understand how the main task translates into a real life situation.

Branding

To redesign the logo, I first mapped out keywords and phrases that best fit the core message of Kaus. I then sketched out around 30 designs and chose three to digitize. The final logo represents fluidity and convenience, with the K shape flowing with just one line.
