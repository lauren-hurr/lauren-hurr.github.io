MediPines

MediPines is a medical device startup that invented the first FDA-cleared portable gas exchange monitor, providing a non-invasive method to measure respiratory health and quickly assess what treatment patients need.

Project Scope

Overview

MediPines’ product, the AGM100, is used by medical staff to check respiratory measurements. The device was initially created because people with obstructive sleep apnea were suffocating in their sleep during post-surgery recovery. Now, the AGM100 is being used to help all patients with respiratory problems to assess what type of treatment they need to ultimately prevent casualties.

I initially learned about this company from an article that described how they were helping patients with COVID-19. I reached out for an opportunity to propose a UX/UI redesign of their website. They were excited to what I would bring to the table.

The Challenge

The client was closely monitoring the website’s analytics and saw that the site traffic has been consistently increasing, yet conversion rates and sales have not shown the same upward trend. Over half of the users visiting the website were mobile users, so it’s very important that this redesign is responsive and works well on mobile.

How might we redesign MediPine’s existing website to provide medical professionals the information they need to ultimately drive more conversion and increase sales?

01.


Research

Speaking With the Client

Client interview

Speaking with the client was integral to understanding the product and empathizing with its users. I needed to understand the significance of this product and its benefits to both patients and medical professionals. I was able to set deadlines, goals, and expectations for this project. The client walked me through the challenges that the company was facing in regards to the growing traffic rate of the website but stagnant conversion rate.

Identifying Current Usability Issues

Heuristic Evaluation, Usability Testing

I conducted a heuristic evaluation using Jakob Nielsen’s 10 usability heuristics to:

  • Help me understand the current usability issues based on established usability design principles
  • Familiarize myself with the current website and learn it as if I was an actual user

Since I’m not the targeted user, and the heuristic evaluation can only uncover around 20% of usability issues, I conducted a usability test with MediPines’ target audience: medical professionals. This ensured that I uncover as many usability issues as I can. I gathered five participants ranging from pulmonologists to registered nurses, and I gave each participant the same scenario and tasks to complete.

02.


Synthesize

Analyzing the Data With the Operations Team

Affinity Mapping, Priority Matrix

Using the data collected from the usability tests, I brought in the operations team to synthesize the findings. We utilized Miro, a collaboration tool, to facilitate this workshop. I wanted the team to understand current usability issues so that they see the purpose behind the redesign choices. We synthesized the findings under four main categories that the client and I decided to focus on - home page, product page, FAQ, and contact page. We then created a priority matrix to identify the most important and feasible problems to tackle.

Pain Points

After we organized the data and determined priorities for the redesign, we decided to focus on the following pain points:

Difficulties understanding the product due to lack of structure within the home page and product page

Difficulties reading text due to lack of contrast and consistency

Difficulties finding the product page, FAQ, contact page, and demo request button

Frustration when filling out the contact form due to lack of structure and unnecessary fields

Clicking non-clickable items due to lack of consistency of colors and buttons

Walls of text are perceived as intimidating

Defining the User

User Persona

After we decided on priorities moving forward, I created a user persona to represent who I will be redesigning the website for. This persona helped guide me to keep the redesigns centered on the user, as I continued to reference it when making design decisions. After I finished the persona, I introduced Michael to the team.

03.


Ideate

User flow

Before starting the designs, I created a user flow of the current MediPine’s website to visualize the steps a user typically takes when visiting the site. I wanted to understand exactly where users were having trouble. I highlighted the areas where I will be focusing on in my redesign, which provided me a clear picture of what I will need to work on.

05.


Validate

Conclusion

Usability Testing, Affinity Mapping

I conducted another usability test with 5 new participants to validate my redesigns based on the research I conducted. The tests were all remote and I used my clickable prototype.

RESULTS

All participants described the website as clean and modern

All participants expressed how they liked how the information was concise and easy to read

All participants were able to find the FAQ and product page within 5 seconds with a 0% error rate.

All participants described filling out the contact and demo form easy and fast

All participants were able to locate the “Request a Demo” button within 3 seconds

Final Hi-Fi Prototype

Homepage

Structured homepage with clear CTA’s to drive users to learn more about the product by visiting the product page or contact MediPine’s to request a demo.

Product Page & Request Demo

Restructured information architecture to showcase information that users would need to know as well as advertise specific aspects of the AGM100, per client request. Users can now easily locate and request a demo by filling out a short form.

FAQ & Contact Page

Accordion FAQ to allow users to have control over the content by only expanding the answers they need.

Contact button is conveniently placed under the FAQ to allow users to directly contact MediPines if they still have unanswered questions.

Reflection

This was the first UX Design project where I got to work with a real world client. This honed my client-facing skills because it was much more collaborative than my previous projects. If I were to re-do this project, I would skip the heuristic evaluation and dive straight into usability testing since I didn’t find the heuristic evaluation very valuable for a website overhaul. However, conducting the heuristic evaluation was a good refresher and made me think about established UX design principles while working on the redesign.

Collaborating with non-designers exercised my ability to advocate for the user, as clearly articulating the pain points made it easier for the client to understand the rationale for the redesign choices. The client’s time was very valuable, so it was also important for me to gather my questions and make the most out of each meeting.
