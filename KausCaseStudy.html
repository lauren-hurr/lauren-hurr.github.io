Overview

Kaus has been a successful insurance provider for over 30 years. However, its growth has stalled recently due to the increased desire by younger customers to move towards digital products. Kaus strives to build a website that makes the difficult insurance buying process more enjoyable.

The Challenge

How might we make the process of buying insurance easier to attract younger customers?

01.


Empathize

What's Wrong with Insurance?

Competitive Analysis, User Interviews, Market Research

The main goal of the research phase was to find out what customers have trouble with when it comes to insurance. I conducted a competitive analysis to uncover competitors' strengths and weaknesses to help establish Kaus’ unique value proposition. Finally, I conducted user interviews with three participants to get an in-depth understanding of people’s experiences purchasing insurance.

Key findings

Most insurance companies only sell insurance through an agent; only 1 company offers users to buy insurance online.

The complexity of products are confusing to inexperienced purchasers.

The abundance of options make the website hard to navigate.

02.


Define

Turning Insights Into Design Opportunities

Empathy Mapping, User Personas, Project goals

After learning more about the insurance industry and users' experiences with the insurance purchasing process, I created an empathy map to help me gain a deeper understanding of the problem space, as well as help translate raw data into actionable insights.

These findings then guided me to build out a user persona. Since insurance products can be confusing and difficult to buy, I created a persona that likes to make informed purchasing decisions by thoroughly understanding the product. I also focused on solving the archaic process of buying insurance by developing a user persona that is tech-savvy and wants a more modernized method. This was used as the basis of my major design decisions.

After I defined the user's goals by creating an empathy map and user persona, I considered business and technical goals to focus on during the design phase.

03.


Ideate

Simplifying the Presentation

Site Map, Card sort

To simplify the navigation process, I began designing the Information Architecture for the top navigation, I conducted a remote card sort using OptimalSort with the help of four voluntary participants. I compared my findings to that of the competitors’ IA and created a site map for the main navigation. I designed it to be easily browsable and familiar.

Defining the Scope

Task Flow, User Flow

I established the scope of my redesign, which is to build out the homepage and the quote process. I created a task flow and user flow using the user persona as the main character. The task flow helped me build out a path that the user would take to purchase insurance. Keeping the user persona in mind, I focused on making the flow intuitive, simple, and short. I broke out decisions and actions in the user flow and minimized the number of tasks in order to improve the “tediousness” of buying insurance.

04.


Prototype

Rebranding and Building a Design System

Hi-Fi Prototype, UI Kit, Logo design

The goal of the UI Kit was to follow a modular approach to ultimately streamline the design process. I also established the brand identity to appeal to a younger consumer base and leave behind the “old school” look of insurance sites. After establishing all the components necessary, I created hi-fi prototypes.

To design the logo, I first mapped out keywords and phrases that best fit the core message of Kaus. I then sketched out around 30 designs and chose three to digitize. The final logo represents fluidity and convenience, with the K shape flowing with just one line.

05.


Test

Testing the Product

Usability Testing, Affinity Mapping

After building out the hi-fi prototype, I conducted remote usability tests with 5 participants between the ages of 24-40. I gave participants scenarios and tasks and asked them to think out loud so I can a better understanding of their thought process. Users would like to know the starting prices for the insurance policies before going through the quote prices.

Next Steps

Next, I have to design, prototype, and test the product pages for the portfolio of insurance products, customizable quote page, and payment page. Another round of usability tests need to be completed after designing these pages.

Reflection

Since this was my first UX design project, I learned about how to apply research methods and design-thinking principles in the context of UX. I believe I spent too much time on micro-interactions, so I was not able to increase the scope for this particular project. For future projects, I will focus on making the prototype simple enough to test the product more quickly so that I can make more iterations within the given time constraint.
