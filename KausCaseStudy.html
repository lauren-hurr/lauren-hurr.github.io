Overview

Kaus has been a successful insurance provider for over 30 years, however, their growth has stalled recently. This is mainly due to the increased desire by younger cutsomers to move towards digital products. Kaus strives to build a website that makes the difficult insurance buying process more enjoyable.

The Challenge

How might we make the process of buying insurance easier to attract younger customers?

01.


Empathize

Understanding the Problem Space

Competitive Analysis, User Interviews, Market Research

I delved deeper into insurance trends and consumer buying behaviors to deepen my understanding of the insurance industry. I then conducted a competitive analysis to uncover competitor’s strengths and weaknesses to help establish Kaus’ unique value proposition. Finally, I conducted user interviews with three participants to get an in-depth understanding of people’s experiences purchasing insurance.

Key findings

Most insurance companies only sell insurance through an agent; only 1 company offers users to buy insurance online.

The complexity of products are confusing to inexperienced purchasers.

The abundance of options make the website hard to navigate.

03.


Ideate

Simplifying the Presentation

Site Map

To simplify the navigation process, I began designing the Information Architecture for the top navigation, I conducted a remote card sort using OptimalSort with the help of 4 voluntary participants. I compared my findings to that of the competitors’ IA and created a site map for the main navigation. I designed it to be easily browsable and familiar.

Defining the Scope

User Flow, Task Flow

I established the scope of my redesign, which is to build out the homepage and the quote process. I created a task flow and user flow using the user persona as the main character. The task flow helped me understand the general path the user is going to take to complete the main task. I broke out decisions and actions in the user flow and minimized the number of tasks in order to improve the “tediousness” of buying insurance.

05.


Test

Testing the Product

Usability Testing, Affinity Mapping

After building out the hi-fi prototype, I conducted remote usability tests with 5 participants between the ages of 24-40. I gave participants scenarios and tasks and asked them to think-aloud so I can a better understanding of their thought process. To synthesize the usability test findings, I used an affinity map to organize the data and found the following issues to be the most prevalent:

Key findings

Users would like to know the starting prices for the insurance policies before going through the quote prices.

Users indicated that they would want to know what they need to give out personal information like their address, since they do not want to receive spam.

Next Steps

Next, I have to design, prototype, and test the checkout process. Usability tests need to be conducted on the new tasks alongside the prototyped quote process above.

Reflection

I believe I spent too much time on micro-interactions, so I was not able to increase the scope for this particular project. For future projects, I will focus on making the prototype simple enough to test additional features more quickly.
